Experience Sri Lanka's Coastal Paradise

About Galle

The capital of the southern province is a city with a energetic history. The spectacular Dutch fort is the most popular attraction of the town which is declared by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site. The 300-year-old Dutch atmosphere is still pretty much alive around the fort and amidst its many historical buildings not invaded by urbanization. Today the town has gradually grown greatly and spreads the best example of fortified city created by Europeans becoming the heart of Galle history.

Top Attractions

  • Sinharaja Rainforest

    Sinharaja Rainforest is Sri Lanka's only tropical rainforest reserve and it is a biodiversity boom of international importance that has been stated as a Biosphere Reserve and a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. More than 70% of the trees and animals are endemic and some of them are considered rare

  • Maritime Museum

    Museum discloses the marine artifacts discovered during explorations made in the ocean floor around Sri Lanka.The artifacts consist of maps, naval craft and articles plus artillery guns, treasuries, beer mugs, smoking pipes, barrels, shoes used by the sailors, and items recovered from the wreckages of ships that sunk in the sea off the Southern coast nearly 800 years ago.

  • Unawatuna Beach

    Unawatuna beach is a great spot to beach bums with plenty of beach properties and fun activities worth engaging.the beach has been arranged with sunbeds,umbrellas,mats which is a heaven for somebody who takes sun dip . On the west side, you can take a wander at the Japanese Peace Pagoda statue overlooking the Indian ocean.the beach is connected with attractive restaurants and pubs.

  • Habaraduwa Turtle Hatchery

    Sri Lanka is home to many turtle hatcheries along the south coast where five-six varieties of turtles visit the shores frequently. The Habaraduwa turtle hatchery looks after the turtles within the radius of Galle and Hambantota districts which serve as nesting habitatat to them, they are taken care and protected from danger of them getting extinction.
