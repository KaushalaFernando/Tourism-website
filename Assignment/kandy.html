About Kandy

Kandy is a major city in Sri Lanka located in the Central Province. The city is situated in the midst of hills in the Kandy plateau, which crosses an area of tropical plantations, mainly tea. Kandy is both an administrative and religious city and is also the capital of the Central Province.

Top Attractions

  • Temple of the Tooth Relic

    The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic or Sri Dalada Maligawa is a Buddhist temple located in Kandy. It is located in the royal palace complex of the former Kingdom of Kandy, which houses the relic of the tooth of the Buddha.

  • Peradeniya Royal Botanical Garden

    This is located about 5.5 km to the west of the city of Kandy in the Central Province of Sri Lanka. It is near the Mahaweli River. The garden includes more than 4000 species of plants, including orchids, spices, medicinal plants and palm trees

  • National Museum of Kandy

    This is located next to the Temple of the Tooth in part of the former Royal Palace of Kandy. The primary exhibits are housed in the Palle Vahala building, which was the former home of the King's harem. A secondary exhibition is located in the main palace building.

  • Lankathilaka Viharaya

    Lankatilaka Viharaya is a Buddhist temple situated in Udunuwara of Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is considered as the most magnificent architectural edifice created during the Gampola era. The vihara buildings have been built on a natural rock called Panhalgala Rock.
